Same wedge, four writers. The fix this round: the line has to answer preview of what? — and the answer is the stuff that isn't your code: prototypes, plans, briefs, mockups, specs.

"Local preview works great — until the work isn't local."

Julie Zhuothoughtful · human · how we work

The work that happens before the product — now shareable.

Most of the real work happens before anything ships — the prototypes, the specs, the briefs, the diagrams. Your coding agent is making all of it. The only problem: none of it was easy to share until the moment it left your machine. protocontent gives every artifact a live URL the instant it's created, so the work can move as fast as the work does.

"The artifacts were always the work. Now they move as fast as it does."

Your agent just finished something — a prototype, a spec, a mockup, a plan. But it's stuck on a machine you're not sitting at. protocontent gives every artifact a live URL the instant it's created, so the work your agent makes on the way to the product is already shareable before you've even asked.

"The artifacts were always the work. Now they have somewhere to live."

Jony Ivecraft · intention · reduction

The work between the work — instantly shareable.

We thought carefully about what gets lost when work leaves a single machine. The prototypes, the plans, the briefs and mockups — the artifacts that surround the code, that carry the intention of the work. They need to travel. protocontent gives each one a live URL the moment it exists, so the work is simply there. For whoever needs it, wherever they are.

"The artifact was always the idea. Now it can go anywhere."

Steve Jobscontrast · inevitability · one image

Local preview stops at your machine. This doesn't.

Here's the problem. Your agent builds prototypes, plans, specs, mockups — all the real working material — and then what? It lives on a machine. Yours, or a cloud server's. So you send a screenshot. You describe it on a call. That's not a workflow — that's a bottleneck. protocontent gives every artifact a live URL the instant your agent makes it. One link. Anyone, anywhere, any device.

"One URL. The work ships itself."
